Meet Our Founders

Peace Batete

Peace Batete, a dedicated Ugandan Pastor and Counselor, leads as the Head Pastor at Urera Ministries International (UMI). Known for her powerful, inspiring sermons, she preaches with compassion and a heart for transformation. A fierce advocate for women's empowerment, family values, and community development, Pastor Peace brings hope to those in prison through counseling, prayer, and encouragement. Her ministry reconciles people to God, mentors believers, and supports prisoners' families, reminding them that God's love offers redemption and restoration to all.

Asaaba Shallot

Asaaba Shallot, a passionate Computer Science student at Women’s Institute of Technology and Innovation (WITU), uses technology to uplift marginalized communities. A key contributor to the innerman pre and primary school website and the Prison Ministry pillar, she inspires young girls and children from single-parent homes with her message of hope and second chances. Through Heart to Heart Prison Ministry, Asaaba supports prisoners in reintegrating into society and provides compassionate care to their families, fostering dignity and restoration.
